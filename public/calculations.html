1. Annual Solar Production

The foundation of all savings calculations starts with estimating how much electricity your solar system will produce annually.

Base Formula:

Annual Production (kWh) = System Size (kW) √ó Sun Hours/Day √ó 365 days √ó Efficiency Factor

Parameters:

  • System Size: Measured in kilowatts (kW) - the rated capacity of your solar panels
  • Sun Hours/Day: Average peak sun hours for your location (typically 4-6 hours)
  • Efficiency Factor: 0.80 (80%) - accounts for real-world losses
Why 80% Efficiency? Real-world solar systems don't operate at 100% efficiency due to:
  • Weather conditions (clouds, rain, snow)
  • Shading from trees or buildings
  • Inverter conversion losses (~4-8%)
  • Temperature effects (panels are less efficient when hot)
  • Wiring and connection losses
  • Dust and dirt accumulation

2. Panel Degradation

Solar panels gradually lose efficiency over time. We account for this in long-term projections.

Year N Production = Base Production √ó (1 - 0.005)^N
  • Degradation Rate: 0.5% per year (industry standard)
  • After 25 years: Panels still operate at ~88% of original capacity

3. Electricity Cost Inflation

Utility rates historically increase over time. We model this to show future savings.

Year N Utility Rate = Current Rate √ó (1 + Inflation Rate)^N
  • Default Inflation: 4% annually (customizable)
  • Historical Average: US electricity rates have increased 2-5% annually over the past 20 years

4. System Offset Percentage

How much of your electric bill is covered by solar production.

Offset % = (Annual Solar Production / Annual Consumption) √ó 100 Annual Consumption = Monthly Bill √ó 12 / Electricity Rate

6. Maintenance Costs
